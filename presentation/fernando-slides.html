Bio

Brooke-Anne is a 1st-year at IE Segovia from Miami. Beginner-level Spanish, online class through IE. She wants to make Spanish-speaking friends and understand Bad Bunny — but the Spanish students at IE don't invite her out because they don't want to switch to English. All of her friends so far are other Americans. Even the waiters in town reply in English the moment they hear her accent.

The problem

The paradox of IE Segovia.

What she has
She's living in Spain. Native speakers in every café, classroom, and corner of Segovia.
Real motivation. She wants to make local friends, understand the culture, stop being "the American girl."
An online IE Spanish class. Vocabulary, grammar, conjugations — all on paper.
What stops her
Spanish students won't switch. They don't invite her out — they don't want to spend the night speaking English just for her.
The waiters give up first. One word of Spanish, an accent, and they reply in English. She never gets to practice.
The American bubble is comfortable. No app, no class, no random encounter has gotten her out of it.
The result
Eight months in Segovia. Still ordering coffee — and making friends — in English.
